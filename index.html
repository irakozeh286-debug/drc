Chapter 1

Introduction

The Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) is located in Central Africa. It is the second-largest country in Africa and one of the richest countries in natural resources. Its capital city is Kinshasa. The official language is French. The DRC shares borders with nine countries and is home to the Congo River, the second-largest river in Africa.

Chapter 2

History

Ancient Kingdoms

Before European colonization, the region was home to powerful kingdoms such as the Kingdom of Kongo, Luba Kingdom, and Lunda Kingdom.

Colonial Period

From 1885 to 1908, the Congo Free State was personally controlled by King Leopold II of Belgium. In 1908 it became the Belgian Congo.

Independence

On June 30, 1960, the country gained independence. Patrice Lumumba became the first Prime Minister.
